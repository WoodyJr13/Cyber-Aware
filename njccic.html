About the NJCCIC

A Resource for New Jersey

State-Level Cyber Hub

The New Jersey Cybersecurity & Communications Integration Cell (NJCCIC) is the U.S.'s first state-run organization dedicated to cyber threat intelligence sharing, attack analysis, and incident response.

Mission & Outreach

It connects public and private sectors by issuing cyber alerts, training materials, and best practices to protect agencies, businesses, and residents.

Free Membership & Resources

NJCCIC offers free membership to individuals, businesses, and organizations. Members get access to weekly threat intelligence reports, cybersecurity webinars, guides on ransomware, phishing, and tools for incident response and protection.

Youth & Student Programs

NJCCIC supports cybersecurity education for young learners across grade levels:

  • Elementary Cyber Safety Patrol (Grades 3–5): A virtual summer program teaching the basics of digital safety.
  • Middle School Cyber Safety Cadet (Grades 6–8): A cybersecurity awareness program promoting safe online behavior.
  • High School Cybersecurity Internships: Includes a 1-week basics internship and an advanced, multi-week program for students interested in cyber careers.
  • College-Level Internships: Hands-on cyber projects and mentorship for undergrads from all majors.
  • CTF Competitions & Events: Includes events like the Garden State Capture the Flag and “Alice in Cyberspace” to teach real-world cyber problem-solving through gamified learning.
